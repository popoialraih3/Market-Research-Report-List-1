<p><p>Unilateral vocal cord paralysis diagnosis encompasses various market types, including laryngoscopy, laryngeal electromyography (LEMG), blood tests, and imaging scans. Laryngoscopy involves direct visualization of the vocal cords to assess their function and identify paralysis. LEMG evaluates nerve and muscle activity in the larynx, providing insights into the cause of paralysis. Blood tests can detect underlying conditions contributing to the paralysis, while imaging scans, such as CT or MRI, help visualize anatomical structures and identify potential lesions or abnormalities affecting the vocal cords.</p></p>
