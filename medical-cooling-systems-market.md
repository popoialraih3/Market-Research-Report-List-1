<p><p>The medical cooling systems market primarily comprises two types: water-cooled chillers and air-cooled chillers. Water-cooled chillers utilize water as a cooling medium, making them efficient for large medical facilities with extensive cooling needs, often requiring cooling towers. In contrast, air-cooled chillers expel heat directly into the air, making them more suitable for smaller facilities or those with space limitations. Both systems are essential for maintaining optimal temperatures in hospitals and labs, ensuring the proper functioning of medical equipment and preserving sensitive materials.</p></p>
